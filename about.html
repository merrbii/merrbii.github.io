Profile

I am an evolutionary biologist studying how genetic variation is generated, maintained, and shaped by evolutionary processes — and how it underpins adaptation in natural populations.

Background

My research sits at the intersection of population and comparative genomics. I combine fieldwork, wet lab experiments, and computational analyses to understand how genomes evolve across different ecological and demographic settings.

Focus

I am particularly drawn to questions about recombination, structural variation, transposable element dynamics, and transitions in reproductive mode — processes that fundamentally reshape genomes and their evolutionary potential.

Study systems

Most of my work centers on invertebrates (e.g., ants, mites, worms, and beetles) chosen because they offer powerful windows into the mechanisms that generate and maintain genetic diversity, while offering the possibility to test and validate theoretical findings experimentally.

Current affiliation

Institute for Evolution and Biodiversity, University of Münster, Germany.
