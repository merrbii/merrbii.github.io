Network

My research is shaped by an international network of collaborators spanning evolutionary genomics, biodiversity, and functional biology.

Parthenogenesis genomics

With Drs. Jens Bast and Philipp Schiffer (University of Cologne), I work on comparative and population genomics of parthenogenesis in worms and mites.

Soil biodiversity genomics

As a member of the Soil Invertebrate Genomics Initiative (SIGI), I collaborate with Prof. Miklós Bálint (Senckenberg Biodiversity and Climate Research Centre) and the other members on genomic resources and evolutionary ecology of soil invertebrates.

Nematode genomics

Through my work on nematodes, I engage with the Tree of Life project at the Wellcome Sanger Institute, on reference genome generation and comparative analyses.

Ant evolutionary genomics

My research on ants spanning social evolution, genome architecture, and adaptation builds on long-standing collaborations with Prof. Jennifer Fewell (Arizona State University), Prof. Guojie Zhang (Zhejiang University), Prof. Jürgen Gadau and Dr. Lukas Schrader (University of Münster), Prof. Jacobus J. Boomsma (University of Copenhagen), and Drs. Eva Schultner and Jan Oettler (University of Regensburg).
